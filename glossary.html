Glossary

The working vocabulary of fuel distribution. Type to filter — it searches terms and definitions.

Basis risk
The gap between the traded hedge benchmark (NYMEX) and the price you actually pay (the local rack). A hedge is never perfect because the thing you can trade isn't exactly the thing you buy.
Bobtail
A small fuel delivery truck (~2,000–4,000 gal) used for last-mile and on-site fills, as opposed to a transport.
Cents per gallon (CPG)
The unit fuel margin is measured in. Distributor gross margin typically runs ~3–6¢/gal.
Contract supply (term)
A committed buying relationship with a supplier at a terminal, usually rack + a small differential, often with volume commitments.
DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid)
A urea-water solution injected into modern diesel exhaust (SCR systems) to cut NOx emissions. A consumable, not a fuel.
Differential
The fixed cents added to or subtracted from a benchmark in a price formula (e.g. "OPIS + 9¢").
Downstream
The consumer-facing end of the oil industry: refining, distribution, marketing, and retail.
Drop size
Gallons delivered at a single stop. Larger drops spread fixed trip cost over more gallons, lowering cost per gallon.
Dyed diesel
Red-dyed off-road diesel — chemically the same as clear ULSD but tax-exempt and legal only for non-road use (construction equipment, generators, heating).
Fixed trip cost
The cost of dispatching a truck (driver, drive time, wear, insurance) — roughly constant regardless of how much fuel is dropped.
Fixed-price contract
A sale at a set price for a period. The customer gets certainty; the distributor takes on price risk.
Hedging
Using a financial instrument (futures, swap, or option) to offset physical fuel price risk on a fixed-price book.
IFTA (International Fuel Tax Agreement)
The system by which interstate truckers report and pay fuel taxes apportioned by miles driven in each state.
Jobber
Traditional term for a fuel distributor — a company that buys at the rack and resells to end users.
Location spread
The price difference for the same product across two terminals on the same day (e.g. NY vs NJ rack).
Motor Fuels Tax
One of New Jersey's two fuel taxes; a fixed per-gallon rate (13.5¢ on diesel).
NYMEX / futures
The exchange where commodity price contracts trade. A "paper" market — few physical barrels change hands; it sets the reference price the rest of the chain follows.
OPIS
Oil Price Information Service — the dominant independent reporting agency for U.S. wholesale (rack) fuel prices. Most contracts reference it.
OPIS timings
The different benchmark times OPIS publishes (Contract, Closing, Calendar Day). Which one a contract cites changes the price.
PPGRT
NJ's Petroleum Products Gross Receipts Tax — a state fuel tax that adjusts annually; rose to 42.6¢ on diesel in Jan 2026.
Rack
The truck-loading point at a terminal — and the name for the wholesale price posted there.
Rack-plus / floating
A sale priced as OPIS + differential, repriced continuously so margin is protected as cost moves. The safer structure for a distributor.
Refiner
A company that converts crude oil into finished products (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel). Owns the largest share of a gallon's cost.
Route density
How tightly delivery stops cluster. High density means more profitable gallons delivered per truck-shift — the biggest controllable profit lever.
SPCC
Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure — an EPA rule requiring a spill plan for facilities storing above-threshold oil quantities.
Spot market
Short-term, high-volume physical fuel trades that sit between the futures market and the rack.
Spot purchase
Buying opportunistically at whichever terminal is cheapest today, rather than from a committed contract.
Spread
Any price difference — by location, product, or time — that drives buying and bidding decisions.
Terminal
A bulk fuel storage facility, usually on a pipeline or waterway, where fuel waits before truck loadout.
Transport
A semi-trailer tanker carrying a full load of roughly 8,000 gallons, as opposed to a bobtail.
ULSD
Ultra-Low Sulfur Diesel — "clear" on-road diesel, ≤15 ppm sulfur. The standard highway product.
